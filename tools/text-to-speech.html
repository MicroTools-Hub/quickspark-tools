    Text to Speech - Convert Text to Natural Audio

    Text-to-Speech (TTS) technology converts written text into spoken audio using synthetic voices that sound increasingly natural. Modern browser-based TTS engines support multiple languages, voices, speech rates, and pitch adjustments, providing accessible audio output without specialized software or server processing.

    Accessibility and Learning Benefits

    Visual Accessibility: Users with visual impairments, dyslexia, or reading difficulties access written content through audio. TTS enables independent consumption of articles, documents, emails, and web pages without assistance.

    Language Learning: Language students hear proper pronunciation and intonation patterns. Adjustable speech rates let learners slow down audio for comprehension or speed up for native-level practice.

    Multitasking: Listen to articles, documents, or notes while commuting, exercising, cooking, or performing other activities. Convert reading time into listening time for productivity.

    Voice and Speed Customization

    Multiple voice options include male and female voices across various languages and accents. Speech rate adjustment (0.5x to 2x speed) accommodates different listening preferences—slower for complex material, faster for familiar content. Pitch adjustment adds further personalization, making audio more pleasant for extended listening sessions.

    Professional applications include proofreading (hearing text reveals errors reading misses), content creation (writers hear how their writing sounds), and accessibility testing (developers verify screen reader compatibility).
